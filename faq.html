TRAVEL TIPS & SAFETY

⚡ Power

Power outlets in Taniti are 120 volts, the same standard used in the United States. American travelers will not need an adapter.

💵 Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its official currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and major credit cards are widely accepted.

🍹 Alcohol

Alcohol may not be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18.

🗣️ Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, particularly by older residents. Plan accordingly if you intend to explore beyond the city.

🏥 Medical

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital employs many multilingual staff members to assist international visitors.

🔒 Safety

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism has increased, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Visitors are advised to keep valuables secure, especially in crowded areas.

📅 National Holidays

Taniti observes a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these days. Check the local holiday schedule when planning your itinerary.
